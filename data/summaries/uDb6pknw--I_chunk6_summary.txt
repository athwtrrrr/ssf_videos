Cultural burning is a traditional practice of starting controlled fires at the right time so that they're cool burn. This type of fire burns only the understory, lowering the fuel density of the forest while allowing time for animals to get out of the way. In Aboriginal culture there isn't really a divide between what is human and what is natural. Scientists are working on how to mitigate this in the future.
