    This workshop on professional networking for STEM students features a panel from journalism, Indigenous research, industry, government, and university career services. Panelists emphasize that strong communication is a critical skill for STEM professionals, highlighting the ability to translate complex information for non-technical audiences and the importance of active listening. Developing this skill takes time and practice, with panelists recommending participation in extracurricular activities, volunteering, and customer service roles to build confidence. They advise students to embrace being a novice, using an analogy of learning to drive a car, which requires patience and accepting mistakes to improve.

Beyond communication, other essential skills for future careers include teamwork, leadership, and effective written communication for job applications. The panel suggests students proactively network by researching organizations, attending professional association events, and using LinkedIn's alumni tool to find contacts. A key strategy recommended is "informational interviewing," where students request brief chats with professionals to learn about their field rather than directly asking for a job. Students are also encouraged to develop a concise "elevator pitch" to introduce themselves effectively in networking situations.
