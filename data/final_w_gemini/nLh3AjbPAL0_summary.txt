    Students presented findings on climate change in Malaysia, covering mangroves, science communication, and the Royal Belum rainforest. The first group highlighted the critical role of mangroves in carbon absorption, noting they store 60% of their carbon in the soil and absorb up to 150 tons per hectare. Despite having the world's third-largest mangrove area, Malaysia has lost 50% of its cover in the last decade due to threats like rising sea levels, coastal development, and pollution. By
