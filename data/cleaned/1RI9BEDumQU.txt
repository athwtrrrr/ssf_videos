The batch leader produces a premium quality compost that has been allowed to mature. The batch leader has three levels. The active, the curing, and the harvest level. In the active phase, there are three bins for the user to drop compost into. When the user has finished in putting what they needed to into the vessel, the user starts the program. The vessel is then locked. The compost heat builds up and then when it drops back below 40Â° C, the bins tip compost into the next level for the curing phase. The lid is unlocked and the bin is ready to be reloaded. The curing phase here, the compost will be topped up by the active vessels until it is full. Once full, the active vessels will start tipping into the alternate curing vessel. Once the alternate curing vessel is also full, the first vessel tips into the harvest level. The at the harvest level, the compost is ready to be used. It can be stored in this space where it will continue to mature like a fine wine. The continuous leader is a simpler design, but has the potential for the mature and the immature compost to mix. The lid on the continuous composter isn't locked, so the user can add little bits when they like. It works by using a labyrinth. The new compost added applies downward pressure in the first cell. When the mixer rotates, the pressure is equalized and the compost is pushed into the next vessel. This is the way that material is pushed from cell to cell through the labyrinth. The labyrinth slows down the flow from start to finish, which reduces the amount of immature compost being mixed with the mature compost. Slowing the flow lets the immature compost build up heat. As the compost decays, it also reduces volume. In this continuous process, there isn't any empty space created by the decay. The material gets pushed along and eventually moves to the end of the process. The user can then harvest the most mature compost by turning the orre at the bottom of the machine.
